Design Principles

Benjamin Herendeen

Alignment

Nike

nike.com

On this Nike page, you can see alignment at it’s best. The heading under the main image is aligned left with the left edge of the image. Both the heading and image line up with the logo on the top. In the navigation on the top, all items are centered vertically. In the image itself, the title and shop button align to the left with padding on the left to push then away from the edge of the image.

Whitespace

Google

store.google.com

With Google’s homepage, you can see whitespace. From the image shown to the shop now button and product tag line. This provides a much more clean look that is professional. Google incorporates whitespace well and is one of the world leaders when it comes to web design.

Contrast

Adobe

adobe.com

For the Adobe homepage, I wanted to focus on the site background with the words and logo. Adobe wants to be seen and convey their message clearly. Since they went with a light-colored background, in this case, white, they need to have darker colors in the foreground. The text is in black and the logo in red. This provides a sharp contrast between the foreground and background making it easier to see the information that is presented.
